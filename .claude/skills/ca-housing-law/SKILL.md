# California Housing Law — Enforcement & Legal Exposure

Companion to policy-analysis skill. That skill provides the analytical framework (how to evaluate decisions). This skill provides California-specific legal substance (what the law requires and what happens when cities violate it).

## Statute Quick Reference

| Law | Code Section | Core Requirement | Penalty for Violation |
|-----|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Housing Accountability Act (HAA)** | Gov. Code § 65589.5 | Cannot deny compliant housing without written findings based on objective standards | $10,000/unit minimum; 5x bad faith multiplier; mandatory attorney's fees |
| **SB 330 (Housing Crisis Act)** | Gov. Code § 66300 | No downzoning below Jan 1, 2018 levels; max 5 hearings on compliant projects | HAA penalties apply; extends through 2030 |
| **SB 35 (Streamlining)** | Gov. Code § 65913.4 | Ministerial approval for compliant projects in cities behind RHNA | No discretionary review; CEQA exempt |
| **SB 79 (Transit Density)** | Effective July 1, 2026 | Tier 1: 120 du/ac, 75ft adjacent; Tier 2: 100 du/ac, 65ft within ¼mi | HAA presumption of violation in high-resource areas (Jan 1, 2027) |
| **Density Bonus Law** | Gov. Code § 65915 | Must grant density bonus + concessions/waivers for projects with affordable units | HAA penalties for denial |
| **Housing Element Law** | Gov. Code §§ 65580-65589.9 | Adequate sites, constraint removal, AFFH, HCD certification | Decertification → Builder's Remedy; AB 1398 rezoning deadlines |
| **Builder's Remedy** | Gov. Code § 65589.5(d)(5) | When housing element noncompliant: city cannot deny projects with ≥20% affordable | Applies automatically; vests at preliminary application |
| **ADU Law** | Gov. Code §§ 65852.2, 66310-66342 | Must approve compliant ADUs ministerially | HCD enforcement; AG referral |
| **Coastal Act** | Pub. Resources Code §§ 30000-30900 | CCC retains discretionary authority over coastal development | Can override density bonus waivers on coastal resource grounds |

## Enforcement Pipeline

```
Violation occurs
    ↓
HCD Technical Assistance letter (voluntary compliance request)
    ↓ (city given 30-60 days to respond)
HCD Notice of Violation
    ↓
HCD referral to Attorney General
    ↓
AG investigation → fines ($10K-$50K/month)
    ↓
Court-ordered compliance / rezoning
```

**Parallel track:** Private enforcement by CalHDF, YIMBY Law, Californians for Homeownership — these orgs sue directly under HAA without waiting for HCD pipeline.

**Coalition pressure track (new as of May 2026):** Six-org coalitions (YIMBY Dems + CalHDF + YIMBY Law + CA YIMBY + Circulate + CFH) now submitting joint demand letters to MPOs (SANDAG) and cities simultaneously — pre-litigation positioning that establishes the legal record and puts cities on notice before suits are filed.

## Penalty Calculator

**HAA violation (project denial):**
- Base: $10,000 × number of units denied
- Bad faith multiplier: 5x (= $50,000/unit)
- Prior violations: additional multiplier
- Mandatory attorney's fees to plaintiff
- Appeal bond required if city appeals (La Cañada Flintridge: $14M bond ordered)

**Example:** Deny 100-unit compliant project in bad faith = $5M minimum + attorney's fees + appeal bond.

**Housing Element noncompliance:**
- Loss of Prohousing Designation (affects grant eligibility)
- Builder's Remedy activated (city loses ability to deny projects with affordable units)
- AB 1398: must complete rezoning within 1 year of deadline or face ministerial approval of compliant projects
- SB 35 streamlining triggered for cities behind RHNA

**SB 79 noncompliance (after Jan 1, 2027):**
- Presumption of HAA violation for denials in high-resource areas (§ 65912.157(m))
- Shifts burden of proof to city
- SANDAG TOD map carries rebuttable presumption of validity (§ 65912.160(f)) — city arguing against its own MPO's map faces uphill battle
- HCD March 2026 advisory: count all trains from all operators; "full-time dedicated transit lane" (not requiring physical exclusivity)

**YIMBY Law litigation record: 9 wins out of 10 lawsuits filed.** Cities almost always lose HAA cases.

## Municipal Obstruction Patterns (CA-Specific)

| Pattern | Mechanism | Legal Vulnerability |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Tier manipulation** | Undercount transit service to classify station as lower SB 79 tier | Train count data from multiple operators; HCD can reclassify |
| **Walking path exclusion** | Require continuous sidewalk for SB 79 walking distance; use own infrastructure neglect as basis | State law defines walking distance, not walking path quality |
| **Coastal Act shield** | Route denials through CCC to override density bonus and state mandates | Tension unresolved; New Commune opened door but didn't close it |
| **Good Cause ≠ rent control** | Classify Good Cause eviction protection as "rent control" to trigger SB 79 exemption | Good Cause eviction (AB 1482) ≠ rent stabilization under Costa-Hawkins |
| **Design review as delay** | Use subjective design standards to impose repeated continuances | SB 330: max 5 hearings; AB 1893: unnecessary delay = disapproval |
| **Post-hoc findings** | Generate objective-sounding denial findings after political decision already made | HAA: findings must be based on objective, pre-existing standards |
| **Self-certified compliance** | City declares own housing element compliant without HCD certification | Martinez v. Clovis: only HCD or court determines substantial compliance |
| **Maximize exemptions** | Draft SB 79 phasing ordinance to exempt maximum parcels | HCD review; likely litigation if exemptions exceed statutory basis |
| **Bus lane disqualification** | Argue bike co-use or right turns negate "dedicated" status to exclude corridors from Tier 2 | Veh. Code § 21655.1 expressly permits both; AB 971 (2023, unanimous) affirmed; SB 79 omits physical exclusivity for bus lanes while requiring it for heavy rail |
| **SANDAG map manipulation** | Lobby MPO to exclude stops from TOD map, reducing SB 79 scope regionally | Map carries rebuttable presumption (§ 65912.160(f)); underinclusive map → HAA liability for cities (§ 65912.157(m)) |

## SB 79 Tier Classification (Common Dispute Point)

**Tier 1** (highest density): Very high-frequency commuter rail (≥72 trains/day across both directions) or heavy rail. Gov. Code § 65912.156(f).

**Tier 2**: High-frequency commuter rail (≥48 trains/day), light rail, or qualifying bus service (full-time dedicated bus lanes + ≤15 min peak frequency). Gov. Code § 65912.156(e), (o).

**Train counting (HCD March 20, 2026 MPO Advisory):** Count ALL trains from ALL commuter rail operators serving the station, across both directions. Multiple services at one station are aggregated — "reflects the overall level of transit access and frequency experienced by riders."

**Red flag:** City classifying a station with 130+ daily trains as Tier 2. Count ALL operators independently (Amtrak, Metrolink, Coaster, SPRINTER don't coordinate responses).

**SANDAG TOD Stop Map:** SANDAG's map carries rebuttable presumption of validity (Gov. Code § 65912.160(f)). Cities relying on underinclusive maps that deny qualifying projects near excluded stops face direct HAA liability beginning Jan 1, 2027 (§ 65912.157(m)). Six-org coalition (YIMBY Dems, CalHDF, YIMBY Law, CA YIMBY, Circulate, CFH) demanded correct designations in May 2026 joint letter.

### "Dedicated Bus Lane" — What Qualifies

SB 79 cross-references only paragraph (1) of Pub. Resources Code § 21060.2(a) — the dedicated-lane + frequency prong. It does NOT import the other 4 BRT requirements (signal priority, all-door boarding, fare collection, defined stations). The threshold is intentionally lower than full BRT.

**Physical exclusivity is NOT required for bus lanes:**
- SB 79 explicitly requires physical exclusivity for heavy rail (Gov. Code § 65912.156(d): "separate rights-of-way from which all other vehicular and foot traffic are excluded") but conspicuously omits this from the bus lane standard (*expressio unius est exclusio alterius*)
- Vehicle Code § 21655.1(b): right turns into transit-only lanes are expressly permitted unless physically separated by curb/fence/barrier
- Vehicle Code § 21655.1(c)(1): local authority may co-designate transit-only lanes for bicycles and other vehicles without defeating dedicated character
- AB 971 (Lee, 2023): passed 77-0 Assembly, 39-0 Senate — unanimously affirmed co-use authorization as clarification of existing law
- SANDAG 2021 Regional Plan Glossary: "dedicated lanes" include those "designated by signs and markings" that "sometimes permit limited use by other vehicles"

**Implication:** Cities cannot exclude bus corridors from Tier 2 by arguing that bicycle co-use or vehicle right turns negate "dedicated" status. This same logic applies to "walking path" fabrication — creative definitions that narrow statutory terms to exclude qualifying parcels.

## Compliance Exposure Assessment

When evaluating a city's legal exposure, check:

1. **RHNA progress** — % of lower-income obligation met at cycle midpoint. Below 50% = SB 35 triggered
2. **Housing Element status** — HCD-certified? If lapsed, Builder's Remedy active
3. **Prohousing Designation** — has it? At risk of losing it?
4. **Recent denials** — any compliant project denied? Each is a potential HAA lawsuit
5. **Downzoning since Jan 1, 2018** — SB 330 violation
6. **SB 79 compliance** — correct tier classifications? Exemptions legally supportable?
7. **Pending litigation** — CalHDF, YIMBY Law, Californians for Homeownership actively suing

**Cumulative exposure compounds.** A city with housing element noncompliance + project denials + SB 330 violations + SB 79 manipulation faces overlapping penalties, multiple plaintiff organizations, and AG enforcement — simultaneously.
