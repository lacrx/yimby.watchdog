## 5. STRONG TOWNS ASSESSMENT

The most fiscally relevant item on this agenda is **Item 8 — Active Transportation Plan grants** totaling up to $650,000 (with a city match of ~$74,555). From a Strong Towns lens, **this is unambiguously good** — an active transportation plan, especially tied to SANDAG's Smart Growth Incentive Program, signals orientation toward walkable, fiscally productive corridors rather than car-dependent sprawl. The city should be pushed to ensure the resulting plan prioritizes the Mission Ave corridor and any SB 79-affected transit corridors for upzoning and infill, not just bike lanes in greenfields. The **Pier demolition emergency work (Item 10, $135K in change orders)** and **6th Aqueduct Pipeline emergency repair (Item 11)** are maintenance of existing infrastructure — appropriate from a Strong Towns perspective, preferable to new sprawl-enabling extensions. No new road widenings or pipe extensions to greenfields are visible on this agenda. **The city is not actively digging a new fiscal hole today, but it's also not building wealth through productive infill approvals.**
